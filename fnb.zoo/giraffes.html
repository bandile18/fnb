Giraffes are fascinating creatures! Here's a wealth of information about these incredible animals:

Physical Characteristics - Height: Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth, with adults reaching heights of 4.6 to 6.1 meters (15 to 20 feet) tall! - Weight: They can weigh between 400 to 900 kilograms (880 to 1,980 pounds). - Neck: Giraffes have long necks, which account for about 6 feet of their height. - Legs: Their legs are also long and powerful, allowing them to run fast and kick predators. - Coat: Giraffes have distinctive coat patterns, which can vary depending on the subspecies.

Habitat and Distribution - Savannas and Grasslands: Giraffes inhabit savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands in Africa. - Range: They can be found in various countries, including South Africa, Kenya, Tanzania, and Mozambique.

Diet and Nutrition - Herbivores: Giraffes are herbivores, feeding on leaves, shoots, fruits, and flowers from trees and shrubs. - Favorite Foods: They particularly enjoy leaves from acacia trees, which are rich in nutrients.

Behavior and Social Structure - Social Animals: Giraffes often live in small groups, called "tows," which usually consist of females and their young. - Male Groups: Males may form bachelor groups or wander on their own. - Communication: Giraffes communicate through a variety of vocalizations, body language, and even scent marking.

Reproduction and Lifespan - Gestation Period: Female giraffes have a gestation period of about 450-460 days (around 15 months). - Calves: Newborn giraffes are about 1.8 meters (6 feet) tall and weigh around 50-70 kilograms (110-154 pounds). - Lifespan: Giraffes can live up to 20-25 years in the wild, although their average lifespan is around 10-15 years due to predation and other threats.
